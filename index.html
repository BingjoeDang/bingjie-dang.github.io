Memristor Devices & Arrays 忆阻器器件与阵列

My research in memristors focuses on developing highly uniform, scalable, and reconfigurable non-volatile memory devices. By engineering ion profiles and exploiting novel switching mechanisms, we aim to overcome the variability challenges in traditional ReRAM.

Specifically, we have demonstrated the integration of hybrid memristor arrays with multi-phototransistors to create light-tunable weights for optic neuromorphic computing.

在忆阻器研究领域，我致力于开发高度均匀、可扩展且可重构的非易失性存储器件。通过离子轮廓工程和探索新型开关机制，我们旨在克服传统阻变存储器（ReRAM）中的可变性挑战。

具体而言，我们展示了混合忆阻器阵列与多光电晶体管的集成，为光学类脑计算创建了光可调权重。

Ferroelectronics & Emerging NVM 铁电电子学与新型非易失性存储器

Ferroelectric materials, particularly HfO2-based thin films (like HZO) and novel nitrides, present a breakthrough for next-generation logic and memory devices. My work involves the growth, characterization, and integration of these materials into advanced device architectures.

A key highlight of my recent research is the Electric-Field-Induced Phase Transition RAM (EPTRAM) based on 3D nitride materials, which provides exceptional thermal robustness and high-speed operation. Furthermore, we have explored HZO/HSO superlattice ReFET arrays for highly stable and efficient non-volatile memory applications.

铁电材料，特别是基于二氧化铪的薄膜（如HZO）和新型氮化物，为下一代逻辑和存储器件带来了突破。我的工作涉及这些材料的生长、表征以及将其集成到先进的器件架构中。

我近期研究的一个关键亮点是基于氮化物材料的电场诱导相变存储器 (EPTRAM)，它提供了卓越的热稳定性和高速运行能力。此外，我们还探索了用于高稳定性和高效非易失性存储器应用的 HZO/HSO 超晶格 ReFET 阵列。

Neuromorphic Computing Hardware 类脑计算硬件系统

Bridging the gap between materials science and artificial intelligence, my work in neuromorphic computing focuses on designing brain-inspired hardware that can process spatiotemporal data (like vision and audio) with extreme energy efficiency.

We have successfully developed hardware implementations of Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) using hybrid memristor arrays featuring fatigue spike-timing-dependent plasticity (F-STDP). Additionally, our reconfigurable optic in-sensor processing architectures allow sensory devices to compute at the edge, drastically reducing data movement overhead.

作为材料科学与人工智能之间的桥梁，我在类脑计算领域的工作重点是设计受大脑启发的硬件系统，使其能够以极高的能效处理时空数据（如视觉和音频信号）。

我们成功开发了基于混合忆阻器阵列的脉冲神经网络 (SNN) 硬件实现，该阵列具有疲劳脉冲时间依赖可塑性 (F-STDP) 特征。此外，我们可重构的光学传感器内处理架构允许传感设备在边缘进行计算，从而大幅降低了数据移动带来的功耗开销。
