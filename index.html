Research Interests

My research focuses on the intersection of advanced materials, emerging memory technologies, and brain-inspired computing architectures. By co-optimizing materials, devices, and algorithms, I aim to develop highly energy-efficient hardware solutions for next-generation AI and 6G applications.

1. Neuromorphic Computing & In-Sensor Processing

  • Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs): Developing hardware implementations of SNNs using hybrid memristor arrays with highly robust learning rules (e.g., fatigue spike-timing-dependent plasticity).
  • Optoelectronic & Audio Synapses: Designing reconfigurable optic in-sensor processing architectures and dynamic memristor-based time-surface neurons for spatiotemporal feature extraction (vision and audio).

2. Emerging Non-Volatile Memory (NVM) & Ferroelectric Devices

  • Phase Transition RAM (EPTRAM): Pioneering thermally robust 3D nitride-based Electric-Field-Induced Phase Transition memory for high-density and high-speed applications.
  • Ferroelectric Electronics: Investigating the ferroelectricity of HZO/HSO superlattices, BFO, and AlScN for highly uniform ReFET arrays and next-generation memory applications.

3. Advanced Micro/Nano-Fabrication & Integration

  • Thin-Film Deposition & Characterization: Extensive expertise in Pulsed Laser Deposition (PLD), Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD), and magnetron sputtering for complex oxide and nitride growth.
  • Emerging Materials Integration: Exploring the integration of Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs) and novel photoluminescent materials into scalable semiconductor device architectures.
